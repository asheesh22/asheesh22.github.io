Research and Project Work:

ü  “Mechanistic Modeling Approach to Continuous Granulation Process in Pharmaceutical Industries”, Research Project, Department of Pharmaceutical Analysis, University of Gent, Belgium.

The project involves understanding the granulation mechanism of pharmaceutical mixture.

Develop theoretical methods to describe multi-dimensional evolution of agglomerates using population balances, flow in dense particle systems; and coupling between flow and population balance models.

Develop a Population Balance Model (PBM) for the development of a population of granules.

Incorporate the PBM into a Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Model to simulate the coalescence and mixing within the twin-screw continuous granulator.

Validation of the model using data obtained from measurements in the process flow.

ü  “Debottlenecking of manufacturing process and utilities consumption in plasma product manufacturing”, Individual Design Project, Sanquin Blood Supply, Division Plasma Products, Amsterdam, The Netherlands

  The project entails a thorough analysis of  all  production steps  and  process equipment, with  emphasis on  the amount of utilities needed.

Modeling of production process resource consumptions so that  profiles becomes more  clear  and  the impact of changes in production planning and changes in the scale of production processes can be predicted.

For that purpose the Intelligen software package “SuperPro Designer” and “SchedulePro” have been used.

During this project detailed economic impacts of existing bottlenecks have  been  studied leading to a techno-economic evaluation of optimised plant.

ü  “Oil palm biomass in Malaysia- How to transform oil palm residues into a business opportunity” Project for PURAC & BE-Basic, The Netherlands along with Malaysian Life Sciences Fund, Malaysia

Current status of  oil  palm  biomass in  Malaysia as  well  as  the  perspective of  using it for  the  production of fermentable sugars was established.

  Together with transportation, the capacity and location of the facilities that process oil palm biomass defined based on logistic approach.

The pre-treatment technologies were compared and the design of two technologies (Dilute acid and Organosolv based) was achieved.

Finally, the economic evaluation of both processes is provided to find  a viable and more  profitable route  for the project.

ü  Process modeling of Pre-treatment of water  hyacinth for pentose sugar  extraction and utilization by Pichia Stiptis in ethanol fermentation (M.Tech Thesis)

  The  goal  of this  work  was  to investigate, both  experimentally and  theoretically by application of mathematical tools, a fermentative system utilizing Water-hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) hemicellulose acid  hydrolysate as a substrate for ethanol production using Pichia stipites

ü  Production of Biodiesel from Crude Oil of Jatropha curcas and Pongamia pinnata (B.Tech. Dissertation)

The study was aimed to produce biodiesel from non-edible oil sources such as crude oil of Jatropha curcas and Pongamia pinnata by transesterification process.

The study concludes that temperature is major parameter influencing the reaction rate and yield of esters.
